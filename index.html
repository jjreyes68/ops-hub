⚠ When the two chains conflict

CAISO dispatches you to 80 MW. Your PPA cap is 75 MW. Follow CAISO. Log the exceedance, notify SC/PSE, document it.

RC issues an emergency directive to reduce output immediately. RC overrides everything — execute first, document simultaneously, notify SC after.

The DP limits interconnection because of distribution voltage issues. That's a reliability constraint — follow it like a TOP curtailment.

When instructions stack up and conflict, this is the order you follow. No exceptions.
1
Emergency / Safety

Immediate threat to life, equipment, or grid stability. Your SOPs and judgment. Nothing overrides this.

2
RC Emergency Directive

The RC has wider-area visibility. Their emergency instruction can override the ISO. Rare — but when it comes, it takes the chain.

3
CAISO real-time dispatch

A DOP or direct CAISO instruction is a reliability obligation. Log it, execute it, document deviations with justification.

4
Transmission constraint (TOP / DP)

TOP curtailment orders, DP interconnection limits — both are reliability constraints overriding your dispatch target.

5
Plant physical limits

Ramp rates, inverter limits, transformer ratings. Never dispatch to a point that damages equipment.

6
PPA / Commercial limits

Last in the reliability stack. Exceeding or missing it due to grid instructions is force-majeure, not a violation on your part.

"Grid first, plant second, contract third. Log everything, call everybody, explain later."
RC Directive

RC issues wide-area emergency curtailment

▾
Setup: Grid event in the Western Interconnection. WECC RC calls CAISO and issues an emergency directive to curtail specific resources including yours. CAISO may not yet have updated your DOP.
1
RC directive received (via CAISO or directly). This is a wide-area stability instruction — highest reliability authority below emergency safety.
2
Execute immediately. Read back per COM-002. Do not wait for your DOP to update — RC instructions act on the grid faster than market signals.
3
Notify SC and log. Timestamp the directive receipt, who issued it, the MW instruction, and your confirmation. This is your paper trail.
4
Monitor for CAISO DOP update. CAISO will catch up — but you executed on RC authority. The two should align quickly.
Operator action: RC authority > CAISO DOP. Execute, read back, notify SC, log everything.
DP Constraint

Distribution Provider limits your interconnection output

▾
Setup: Your plant interconnects at distribution voltage. The DP calls with a curtailment — local distribution feeder is overloaded and voltage is out of band. Your CAISO DOP is 50 MW but the DP says hold to 30 MW.
1
DP curtailment received. This is a distribution-level reliability constraint. Treat it like a TOP curtailment — it's a physical limit on what the grid can safely absorb at your POI.
2
Confirm and read back. "Confirmed, holding to 30 MW, feeder voltage constraint." Log time and who issued.
3
Execute curtailment. You are now deviating from your CAISO DOP — this needs to be documented.
4
Notify CAISO of deviation. Log the DOP vs actual discrepancy and the reason (DP constraint, feeder name/ID). CAISO state estimator needs to know.
5
Notify SC/PSE. Lost 20 MW — commercial position is affected. SC needs to adjust their market exposure.
Operator action: DP limit is a reliability constraint. Follow it, notify CAISO, then notify SC.
PPA Conflict

CAISO dispatches below PPA minimum

▾
Setup: PPA requires 60 MW minimum. CAISO sends a 40 MW DOP due to local congestion.
1
CAISO sends DOP: 40 MW. This lands in your EMS as a new dispatch target.
2
Execute. Ramp to 40 MW per your unit's ramp rate. Do not hold at 60 MW "for the PPA."
3
Notify SC/PSE immediately. Call or log that a CAISO curtailment is active and you are below PPA minimum. Give them MW and timestamp.
4
Document everything. DOP receipt time, ramp, PPA deviation, notification. This is your force-majeure paper trail.
Operator action: Follow CAISO, notify SC, document. The commercial fix is not your problem to solve in real time.
